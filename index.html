Pharmaceutical Companies and Mental Health



Sabrina Li

Abstract

There exists an issue concerning pharmaceutical companies gaining profit through the administration and purchase of drugs to medically treat patients. Through research on the side effects of pharmaceutical drugs prescribed for mental illnesses, this study aims to spread awareness about the ways mental health patients are negatively impacted due to pharmaceutical companies focusing on ways to earn more money and caring less about improving the condition of patients. The areas of interest studied consist of pharmaceutical drug side effects on patients and their effectiveness in treating mental illnesses. The results of this research are conveyed through a creative work and write up representing the relationship between pharmaceutical companies, psychiatric medication, and mental health patients. Data have been collected from articles and research books. This study challenges the arguments that pharmaceutical companies focus on helping patients get better and that the drugs they currently produce are sufficient in controlling the symptoms of people with mental health issues. Although medication is often complemented with therapeutic treatments to increase effectiveness, the side effects that come with certain pharmaceutical drugs can cause more stress and suffering that may add to already existing mental illness symptoms. Furthermore, medication does not always help patients and they often experiment with multiple drugs in hopes of finding one that works for them. Patients are also not guaranteed to return to normal functioning or feel their pain alleviated despite putting up with side effects from their medication and the long treatment process.
